OBSERVATIONS

PART A: Preparation of standard solution of disodium salt of EDTA

Weight of weighing bottle + EDTA salt, W1 = g

Weight of empty weighing bottle, W2 = g

Weight of EDTA transferred, (W1 - W2) = g

Molarity of EDTA = (Weight of EDTA salt / Molecular weight of EDTA) × 4

Molarity of EDTA = ( / 372.24 ) × 4 = M (X)

PART B: Determination of total hardness of water sample

Burette: Standard EDTA solution

Conical flask: 25 cm³ of water sample + 5 cm³ of NH3–NH4Cl buffer solution

Indicator: Eriochrome black-T.

Endpoint: Wine red to clear blue.

Burette reading I II III
Final Burette reading
Initial Burette reading
Volume of EDTA run down in cm³

Concordant burette reading = cm³ (Y)

CALCULATIONS:

1000 cm³ of ‘X’ M EDTA = 100g of CaCO3 (Molecular mass of CaCO3 = 100)

⇒ ‘Y’ cm³ of ‘X’ M EDTA = ?

= (100 × Y × X) / 1000 = g of CaCO3 (Z)

25 cm³ of hard water sample contains g of CaCO3 (Z)

⇒ 106 cm³ of hard water sample contains = (Z × 106) / 25 = ppm of CaCO3

= ppm of CaCO3

RESULT:

Total hardness of the given water sample = ppm of CaCO3
